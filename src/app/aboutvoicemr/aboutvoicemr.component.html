VoICE-MR

West Virginia University’s (WVU) Center for Alternative Fuels, Engines and Emissions (CAFEE), Clean Cities Coalition (CCC) from the regions of West Virginia, Coachella Valley, CA, Pittsburgh region and the Western Riverside Council of Governments, CA and Wale Associates Corporation. jointly propose a study to develop a tool to estimate the vocation dependent variations in maintenance cost (MC) of heavy- and medium-duty vehicles fueled by alternative fuels that include natural gas, propane, and electric. South Coast Air Quality Management District (SCAQMD) and Southern California Gas Company (SoCal Gas) have jointly approved a cash contribution towards this proposed study and will be a key partner in this study. SCAQMD and SoCal gas believe that the outcomes of the study will have significant impact in increasing the awareness and acceptance of alternative fueled vehicles (AFV), in-specific natural gas vehicles in the Southern California region. Vocation Integrated Cost Estimate (VoICE) for maintenance and repair (MR) to compare cost estimates of AFV with conventional diesel vehicles of the same vocation to illustrate vocation-specific benefit of adopting AFVs.

The proposed study will in specific address medium- and heavy-duty vehicles operating in urban delivery, port drayage, school buses, refuse trucks and transit bus applications. This comprehensive study has enabled a close partnership of WVU CAFEE with several port drayage truck fleets, food distribution, refuse, transit agencies, school districts and beverage distribution fleets. These fleet partners operate a combination of natural gas, propane, electric and diesel-powered vehicles of different model years, vehicle manufacturers and technology development levels.
